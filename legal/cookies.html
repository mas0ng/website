Cookie notice

Cookies and browser storage.

This notice explains the limited cookies and browser storage used by public pages, sign-in, and site tools.

Last updated: 11 July 2026

Public pages

Public pages do not use cookies or similar technologies for analytics, advertising, behavioural tracking, or tracking across websites.

After you select “Dismiss notice”, informational public pages store one local value so the notice stays dismissed. It does not identify an account or record browsing for advertising or analytics.

The privacy notice

Selecting “Dismiss notice” stores mas0ng_privacy_notice_acknowledged_v1 in local storage until it is cleared or replaced. This records a user-interface preference; the notice is informational and the button is not a request for consent.

Sign-in cookies

The sign-in page first shows a full-screen explanation. Continuing acknowledges the explanation; it is not consent to optional tracking. The security technologies below are necessary to provide the private sign-in service and are not used for advertising or analytics.

  • mas0ng_session (or the configured equivalent) is an HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Lax authentication cookie. It normally expires after seven days, or earlier on logout, revocation, or security invalidation.
  • mas0ng_session_bootstrap is an HttpOnly, Secure, SameSite=Lax verification cookie used to make a newly issued session available safely across mas0ng.com subdomains. It expires after 90 seconds.
  • Short-lived server-side login challenges expire after five minutes; QR login records expire after two minutes; failed-attempt rate-limit records expire after 15 minutes.

Public pages may send an existing session cookie to the authentication service to determine whether the browser is already signed in. An anonymous check does not create a session.

Cloudflare Turnstile

The sign-in page loads Cloudflare Turnstile only after the required sign-in notice is acknowledged. The security-report page loads it only when you choose to open the report form. Turnstile may store or access information and process browser, device, network, and challenge signals needed to distinguish legitimate requests from automated abuse. See Cloudflare's privacy policy.

Tools and protected apps

Some tools store information in local or session storage when you request their features, such as timer state, temporary console output, interface preferences, music preferences, caches, or draft state. Protected apps may also hold temporary browser state while a feature is in use. This information stays in the browser unless the relevant feature says it will be uploaded or saved to an account. Its duration depends on the feature: session storage ends with the browser session; local storage remains until the feature replaces it or you clear it.

No analytics or advertising storage

The current public pages do not set analytics, advertising, or behavioural tracking cookies. If that changes, this notice and the site's controls will be updated and consent will be requested where UK law requires it.

Your controls

You can clear cookies, local storage, and session storage through your browser settings. Clearing authentication cookies signs you out. Blocking the strictly necessary sign-in technologies means the private service cannot authenticate or protect a session. Public pages remain available.
