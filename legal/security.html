Security notes

Keeping private apps protected.

These notes explain intended access controls, session checks, server-side secrets, upload limits, and how to report security concerns.

Last updated: 9 June 2026

Authorised access

Private pages and Worker API routes are intended for authorised users only. Browser sign-in is handled at auth.mas0ng.com. Session cookies, server-side auth verification, and route checks protect private areas.

Sign-in monitoring and automated analysis

Sign-in and protected requests may be logged, rate-limited, challenged, or blocked. This can include IP addresses, timestamps, user agents, request paths, failed login reasons, session checks, and related security metadata.

Automated systems — including AI-assisted analysis — may be used to review login attempts and request patterns for unusual behaviour, brute-force activity, suspicious timing, or other signs of abuse. These checks are intended to keep private areas secure, not to profile ordinary public visitors.

Before signing in, you must agree to this monitoring notice on the login page. If you do not agree, access to protected areas should be refused.

Cloudflare Turnstile

The login page may use Cloudflare Turnstile to help distinguish genuine sign-in attempts from automated abuse. Turnstile may process browser and request signals according to Cloudflare's own terms and privacy practices.

AI token protection

AI provider keys should stay server-side in Worker secrets. The browser should call the private AI Worker, and the Worker should verify the session before sending requests to providers such as OpenAI or DeepSeek.

Upload limits

The AI app enforces compressed file limits, total upload allowance, same-origin checks for unsafe requests, and retention rules for pinned and recent chats. These controls reduce storage abuse and accidental cost exposure.

Session safety

  • Keep login details private.
  • Sign out when using a shared device.
  • Clear browser cookies if a device is lost or no longer trusted.
  • Report unexpected login, session, AI usage, upload, or dashboard behaviour.

UK applicable law

These notes are written for users in the United Kingdom and are intended to align with UK online and data protection law, including the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, PECR, the Online Safety Act 2023 where relevant, and related computer misuse and fraud offences that may apply to unauthorised access attempts.

mas0ng.com is a personal site with private authorised access. It is not operated as a large public user-to-user platform, but unlawful, harmful, abusive, or exploitative use remains prohibited under UK law and the site Terms of Use.

Reporting concerns

If you believe you have found a security issue, contact mason@mas0ng.com with enough detail to understand and reproduce the issue.
