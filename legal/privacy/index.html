Privacy policy

How mas0ng.com uses personal data.

This policy explains what information is handled when you visit public pages, sign in, or use protected features, and the choices and rights available to you.

Who is responsible

Mason, the UK-based operator of mas0ng.com, is the controller responsible for personal data handled through this service. Questions and data protection requests can be sent to mason@mas0ng.com.

What this policy covers

This policy covers mas0ng.com public pages, sign-in, protected apps, AI features, uploads, and related security and support communications. Separate policies apply to individual Discord bots. External websites, including social platforms, control their own processing after you choose to open them.

Public pages and external resources

Public pages do not use third-party analytics, advertising, or behavioural tracking. They do make ordinary requests needed to deliver the site and public content. Hosting and security providers receive standard request information such as IP address, user agent, requested URL, and time.

The site loads a body font from Google Fonts. This causes the browser to contact Google and disclose standard request information, including the browser's IP address.

Public pages may ask the authentication service whether the browser already has a valid session. This request uses any existing session cookie but does not create a session for an anonymous visitor.

Cookies and browser storage

Informational public pages store one local value after “Dismiss notice” is selected so the notice stays dismissed. It is not used to track behaviour across websites and the button is not a consent control.

After a successful sign-in, essential cookies keep the session authenticated. Public pages may use those cookies to check whether the browser is already signed in.

More information is in the Cookie Notice.

AI processing

When an AI feature is used, the prompt, relevant conversation context, selected settings, and readable attachment content may be sent to OpenAI or DeepSeek to generate a response. Images and PDF files may be sent to OpenAI to extract readable text even when a different provider is selected for the conversation. Extracted text may then be included in a later request to the selected model provider.

Chats and files may be saved so they can be reopened. Files are retained for pinned chats and a limited number of recent unpinned chats; older files may be deleted while chat text remains. You can delete chats through the app. See the AI Policy for more detail.

Lawful bases

Where UK GDPR applies, personal data is processed under the following lawful bases:

  • Legitimate interests: delivering and maintaining the site, providing requested private features, protecting accounts and services, preventing misuse, and responding to enquiries. These interests are balanced against the limited and expected nature of the processing.
  • Legal obligation: where information must be retained, disclosed, or otherwise handled to comply with law.
  • Consent: if a future feature requires consent, including any non-exempt storage or access technology. The current public pages do not use advertising or analytics storage.

Where information comes from and whether it is required

Information normally comes directly from you, from your browser or device when it connects, from Cloudflare's delivery and security services, or from a connected service you deliberately use. Security alerts and logs may derive approximate country information and risk signals from network data.

Public pages can be viewed without an account. Account identifiers, authentication data, essential cookies, Turnstile signals, and basic security records are required to sign in and use protected services. If they are not provided or are blocked, sign-in cannot be completed. AI prompts and files are optional, but the requested AI feature cannot process content that is not supplied.

Who receives information

  • Cloudflare provides hosting, content delivery, Turnstile, security, and related operational services. See Cloudflare's privacy policy.
  • Google supplies the Google Fonts resource used on public pages. See Google's privacy policy.
  • OpenAI and DeepSeek process content when their AI services are used; OpenAI may also process supported files for text extraction. See the providers' OpenAI privacy policy and DeepSeek privacy policy.
  • Discord may receive sign-in security alerts containing the outcome, IP address, approximate country, user agent, requested destination, and a shortened account hash, and receives developer-site security reports submitted through the reporting form. Discord also processes data for Discord bots under their separate policies. See Discord's privacy policy.
  • Other connected services receive information only when an authorised user asks a protected feature to use that service.
  • Regulators, law enforcement, advisers, or other parties may receive information where required by law or necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal rights.

Personal data is not sold and is not used for advertising.

International processing

Cloudflare, Google, OpenAI, DeepSeek, Discord, and other connected providers may process personal data outside the United Kingdom. Such transfers must be covered by an applicable UK transfer mechanism, such as UK adequacy regulations, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement or Addendum, or another legally permitted safeguard. You can ask for information about the relevant safeguard by emailing the address above.

Retention

  • The public privacy-notice preference remains until you clear it or a later version replaces it.
  • Normal sessions expire after seven days; the cross-subdomain bootstrap cookie expires after 90 seconds. Either can end sooner on logout, revocation, or security invalidation.
  • Login challenges expire after five minutes, QR login requests after two minutes, and failed-attempt rate-limit records after 15 minutes. Provider security and delivery logs may have separate retention periods.
  • AI chats remain until deleted. Attached files remain for pinned chats and the five most recent unpinned chats by default; older unpinned attachments may be removed while chat text remains.
  • Contact messages are kept as long as needed to respond and maintain an appropriate record.
  • Service providers retain their own records under their applicable settings and policies.

Security and automated checks

Reasonable technical and organisational measures are used to restrict access and protect personal data. Sign-in and protected requests may be logged, rate-limited, challenged, allowed, or blocked using session validity, challenge results, request patterns, and other security signals.

These checks protect private areas and are not used to profile ordinary public visitors or to make legal or similarly significant decisions about them. No internet service can guarantee absolute security.

Your rights

Depending on the circumstances, UK data protection law may give you rights to be informed; access, correct, or erase personal data; restrict or object to processing; obtain portable data; and challenge certain solely automated decisions. Consent can be withdrawn where processing relies on consent. These rights are not absolute and identity may need to be verified.

Send a request to mason@mas0ng.com. Requests are normally answered within one month, subject to lawful extensions or exemptions. You can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office.

Children

mas0ng.com is not directed at children and is not intended to knowingly collect children's personal data. Contact mason@mas0ng.com if you believe a child has provided personal data.
