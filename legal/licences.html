Software Licensing

We release software, tools, and assets under various licensing models depending on the project. Here is an overview of what each license is and what you can and cannot do.

Last updated: 11 July 2026

Overview

Software repositories, packages, and assets should specify their terms in an accompanying LICENSE, COPYING, notice, or other licence file. The exact licence text supplied with an item controls. The summaries below are general guidance only and do not grant rights or replace the applicable licence. If no licence is specified, copyright law applies and no additional permission is granted.

MIT License (Permissive Open Source)

A highly permissive, lightweight open-source license. It allows developers to reuse and modify code with minimal restrictions.

  • What you can do:
    • Use the code commercially.
    • Modify the codebase.
    • Distribute modified versions of the software.
    • Sublicense the software to third parties.
    • Use the software privately.
  • What you cannot do:
    • Hold the author liable (the software is provided "as is" without any warranties).
  • What you must do:
    • Include the original copyright notice and MIT license text in all copies or substantial portions of the software.

GNU GPL v3 (Strong Copyleft Open Source)

A strong copyleft license designed to ensure that the software (and any derivative works) remains free and open-source for all users.

  • What you can do:
    • Use the software commercially.
    • Modify the codebase.
    • Distribute the software and its derivatives.
    • Run and use the software privately.
  • Important limits:
    • When a covered work is conveyed, the GPL's source, notice, and corresponding-source requirements may apply to the covered work and qualifying derivatives.
    • Whether particular combined software is a derivative or aggregate is fact-specific; consult the exact GPL text and obtain advice when needed.
    • Hold the authors liable for damages.
  • What you must do:
    • Disclose the source code when distributing modifications or derivatives.
    • Include the original copyright notice, copyleft notice, and license text.
    • State any changes made to the original files.

Apache License 2.0 (Permissive with Patent Grant)

A permissive open-source license similar to MIT, but with explicit provisions for patent grants and trademark limitations.

  • What you can do:
    • Use the code commercially, modify it, distribute it, and sublicense it.
    • Use, practice, and transfer patent rights granted by contributors.
  • What you cannot do:
    • Hold authors liable for warranties or damages.
    • Use the trademarks, service marks, or names of the project/contributors.
  • What you must do:
    • Include the original license, copyright, and patent notice.
    • Add a prominent notice in modified files stating that you changed them.
    • Include a copy of the NOTICE file if the original repository has one.

Proprietary & Closed Source (Hosted Apps & Premium Assets)

Private applications, APIs, assets, and backend services may be proprietary. Access to a hosted service does not by itself grant a licence to copy or distribute its underlying software.

  • What you can do:
    • Access and use the software/service through our authorized platforms.
    • Use an authorised hosted feature for the purposes and within the limits shown for your account.
  • What you cannot do:
    • Decompile, reverse engineer, or attempt to extract source code except where applicable law gives a right that cannot be excluded.
    • Redistribute, copy, rent, lease, or sub-license the software.
    • Modify, bypass, or tamper with security mechanisms or licensing verification controls.
    • Claim ownership or authorship of the software.
  • What you must do:
    • Comply with our Terms of Use and respect access/usage limitations.

Creative Commons (CC BY-NC-ND) (Assets & Media)

Typically applies to design templates, visual media, graphics, and documentation. CC BY-NC-ND stands for Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives.

  • What you can do:
    • Download, store, and share the material with others.
    • Use the material for personal, non-commercial purposes.
  • What you cannot do:
    • Use the material for commercial purposes or monetization.
    • Alter, remix, transform, or build upon the material (no derivatives).
    • Share the material without proper attribution.
  • What you must do:
    • Provide appropriate credit to the author (Mason) and link to the source and license.

Default: All Rights Reserved

If a repository, asset, or file does not carry an explicit license, it defaults to a standard copyright reservation.

  • What you can do:
    • View material made publicly available and exercise rights that copyright law or the hosting platform's terms expressly permit.
  • What you cannot do:
    • Copy, modify, redistribute, sublicense, or use the code in other products/services without explicit written permission.
